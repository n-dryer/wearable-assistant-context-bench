Wearable Assistant Context Benchmark

Tests whether multimodal assistants follow the situation in front of them or get stuck on the one a turn ago. Built to pick a model for a live wearable assistant.

Cross-family judging correction

When a model judges itself, it scores higher

Original → Run A delta
−5.7 pp

Same Gemini candidate, two different judges. Gemini judging Gemini gives it 98.5%. GPT-4o-mini judging the same Gemini gives it 92.8%. The 5.7 point gap is a model rating its own family more favorably. Cross-family judging is the default here so this can't happen quietly.

Camera channel ablation

The camera channel does the work

Run A vs Run C delta
−85.6 pp

Turn the camera off and the same Gemini candidate drops from 92.8% to 7.2%. Without the camera, the model has nothing to work with: the user is asking "is this right?" and never says what "this" is. The camera is carrying the test.

The test, in plain English

Picking the right frame, turn by turn

Three-turn conversations. Between Turn 1 and Turn 2 the user's situation changes. They swap tools, walk into a different room, switch what's on a screen, that kind of thing. The change shows up in the camera; the user doesn't announce it out loud. When they ask a follow-up like "am I doing this right?", does the model answer about what's in the camera now, or about what was there a turn ago?

Design details are in benchmark_spec. How to read scores and what's out of scope is in benchmark_notes. The one-page summary card is at benchmark_card or PDF.

What it doesn't test

Out of scope

  • Advice quality. The judge doesn't check whether the answer is correct, safe, or appropriate to the domain. A confidently wrong answer can pass.
  • Real video. The "camera" is text descriptions of what the camera would see, not actual frames. A model that does well here might not do well on real video.
  • Live audio and voice mode. Scoring is on text. A model that ships in a real wearable also needs to listen, talk back, and handle interruptions in real time. None of that is exercised here.
  • Multi-turn dynamics, proactive coaching, domain depth, latency, cost, audio perception, speaker attribution, addressee detection, long-horizon memory. Out of scope. Full list in benchmark_notes.
